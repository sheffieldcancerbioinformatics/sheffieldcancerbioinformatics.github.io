## Lewis Quayle BSc (Hons.), MRes, PhD, FHEA

<img src="/images/people/lquayle.png" width="35%" style="display: block; margin: 0 left;">

### **Senior Lecturer in Data Science & Analytics | Cancer Bioinformatician**

## Overview

Dr. Lewis Quayle is a cancer biologist, bioinformatician and data scientist with a background that combines experimental and computational oncology. Currently, he serves as a Senior Lecturer in Data Science and Analytics at Sheffield Hallam University and holds an honorary position as a Senior Lecturer in Bioinformatics at the University of Sheffield.

Lewis trained in experimental oncology, completing his PhD at the University of Sheffield in 2018 on the identification and characterisation of mitotically quiescent sub-clones in breast cancer. He subsequently transitioned into computational oncology, combining wet-lab expertise with large-scale omics analyses.

His research sits at the interface of cancer biology, bioinformatics, and data science, using multi-omic profiling, computational modelling, and high-performance analytics to study metastasis, tumour dormancy, and therapeutic resistance. His independent programme focuses on preventing metastatic relapse and improving stratified treatment approaches in high-incidence malignancies.

He develops scalable bioinformatics pipelines and reproducible analytical frameworks for multi-omic cancer research, integrating statistical modelling and machine learning to identify biomarkers and clinically actionable insights. His work contributes to national precision medicine initiatives, including Genomics England’s <a href="https://www.genomicsengland.co.uk/initiatives/100000-genomes-project" target="_blank">**100,000 Genomes Project**</a> and the <a href="https://fundingawards.nihr.ac.uk/award/NIHR128103" target="_blank">**GUSTO phase II clinical trial**</a>, where he contributes to mutational landscape profiling and molecular subtyping efforts in bladder and ovarian cancers.

Alongside research, Lewis supervises doctoral and postgraduate researchers and is committed to open and reproducible science. He serves on the Editorial Board of <a href="https://link.springer.com/journal/10585" target="_blank">**Clinical & Experimental Metastasis**</a>, is a steering committee member of the <a href="https://northernbug.github.io/" target="_blank">**Northern Bioinformatics User Group**</a>, and contributes to international reproducible research initiatives including <a href="https://northernbug.github.io/" target="_blank">**nf-core**</a>. He is a Fellow of Advance HE and a certified <a href="https://carpentries.org/" target="_blank">**The Carpentries**</a> instructor.

## Research

Lewis’s research focuses on translational cancer bioinformatics, integrating multi-omic profiling, computational modelling, machine learning, and high-performance analytics to address metastasis, tumour dormancy, therapeutic resistance, and challenges in precision oncology. More broadly, his work spans next-generation sequencing and mass spectrometry, high-throughput screening analytics, public healthcare data science, and reproducible bioinformatics pipeline development, with a consistent emphasis on converting complex biological data into clinically actionable insight.

He contributes to national precision medicine initiatives through Genomics England’s <a href="https://www.genomicsengland.co.uk/initiatives/100000-genomes-project" target="_blank">**100,000 Genomes Project**</a>, where he is a member of the Clinical Interpretation Partnerships in bladder and ovarian cancer, contributing to mutational profiling and molecular subtyping analyses. He is also part of the bioinformatics team for the <a href="https://fundingawards.nihr.ac.uk/award/NIHR128103" target="_blank">**GUSTO phase II clinical trial**</a>, supporting genomic stratification and treatment-response modelling in muscle-invasive bladder cancer.

His independent research programme centres on identifying molecular drivers of metastatic dormancy and therapy resistance, with the aim of preventing relapse and improving stratified treatment approaches in high-incidence malignancies.

Notable current collaborative projects include predictive modelling of EV-derived microRNA-driven microbiotal dysbiosis in colorectal cancer, biomarker discovery and validation through advanced survival analysis framework development, and translational applications of multi-omic data integration. He also supports various smaller projects in data analytics, public healthcare data, text mining and sentiment analysis, and predictive modelling.

## Teaching & Supervision

Lewis has extensive experience teaching and supervising across biomedical and computational disciplines. He began supervising research projects during his MRes and has since supported undergraduate, BMedSci, MSc, and doctoral students across oncology, bioinformatics, and data science.

He previously lectured on next-generation sequencing, bioinformatic analytics, and reproducible computational research within the MSc Translational Oncology and MSc Molecular Medicine programmes at the University of Sheffield. At Sheffield Hallam University, he lectures within the Digital Analytics and Technologies subject group and currently develops and leads the MSc Data Analytics Tools and Techniques module. He previously led the MSc modules Learning Systems and Data Analysis for Healthcare and Ethics of Healthcare Information, and contributes to the Advanced Data Management Project module on the MSc Big Data Analytics programme.

He has been awarded Fellowship of Advance HE (FHEA) in recognition of his alignment with the Professional Standards Framework (2023) and is a certified <a href="https://carpentries.org/" target="_blank">**The Carpentries**</a> instructor, delivering training in reproducible coding, data science, and research software practices.
