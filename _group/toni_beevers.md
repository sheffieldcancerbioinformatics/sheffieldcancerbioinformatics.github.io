Toni is a postdoctoral researcher with expertise in cancer supportive care, scalp cooling, chemotherapy-induced alopecia, patient-reported outcomes, and applied data analysis. Her doctoral research investigated the factors influencing the efficacy of scalp cooling in preventing chemotherapy-induced alopecia, using large-scale real-world patient-reported data. Her work now, which is a joint project with <a href="https://profiles.shu.ac.uk/10030-nik-georgopoulos" target="_blank">Dr Nik Georgopoulos</a> (line manager), focuses on improving understanding of scalp cooling outcomes, supporting patient-centred care, and developing evidence to inform clinical practice and patient counselling.

## Education

### **PhD | University of Huddersfield**
*2022 – 2025*

- Thesis: "*Investigation into the parameters influencing the efficacy of scalp cooling to prevent chemotherapy-induced alopecia*".
- Investigated the incidence of chemotherapy-induced alopecia in patients using scalp cooling during chemotherapy.
- Analysed real-world patient-reported data from the Dutch Scalp Cooling Registry, a multi-cohort database of patients in the Netherlands collected between 2006 and 2019.
- Explored determinants of scalp cooling efficacy, including chemotherapy regimen, treatment factors, demographics, hair-related characteristics, and patient experiences.
- Produced clinically relevant evidence to support improved patient counselling, treatment personalisation, and decision-making around scalp cooling use.
